TITLE: Highly Skilled Migrant - Salary Criteria and MVV Entry Visa
SOURCE_URL: https://business.gov.nl/coming-to-the-netherlands/permits-and-visa/residence-permit-for-highly-skilled-migrant/

To qualify as a highly skilled migrant, the applicant must meet the Netherlands' general residence permit requirements (valid passport, no threat to public order or national security) plus scheme-specific conditions. The employer must be a recognised sponsor, and the employment contract must be between that sponsor and the employee.

A central requirement is salary: the employee's gross monthly pay must be competitive with the Dutch market rate and meet one of three official minimum salary thresholds, which are adjusted annually. A reduced ("young graduate") threshold applies to people who held or qualified for the orientation-year permit and are applying within three years of graduating or finishing their research.

Depending on nationality, the employee may also need a provisional residence permit, known by its Dutch abbreviation MVV (machtiging tot voorlopig verblijf) - effectively an entry visa allowing a stay longer than 90 days. Where required, the recognised sponsor applies for the MVV and the residence permit together, so the applicant does not need to file two separate requests. The residence permit itself is granted for the duration of the employment contract, up to a maximum of five years; after five years, the employee may become eligible to apply for permanent residence.

Some conditions are nationality-specific: Turkish nationals, for example, are exempt from the requirement that their employer be a recognised sponsor, due to a separate association agreement.
