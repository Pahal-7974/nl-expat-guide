TITLE: Applying for DigiD From Outside the Netherlands
SOURCE_URL: https://www.netherlandsworldwide.nl/digid-abroad/how-to-apply
SOURCE_URL_2: https://www.denhaag.nl/en/moving-and-immigration/apply-for-a-digid-if-you-live-abroad/

Someone who lives outside the Netherlands but already holds a Dutch BSN (for example because they're in the process of moving, or are registered in the Non-Residents Records Database, RNI) can still apply for a DigiD before physically arriving. The process differs from the standard domestic route because there's no Dutch postal address to send an activation letter to.

After completing the online application, the applicant receives a "collection code" - a 9-digit code starting with the letter B - sent by both text message and email. This collection code is valid for 30 days and is not itself the activation code; it's what's needed to retrieve the actual activation code in a second step. To get the activation code, the applicant either visits a physical DigiD service desk in the Netherlands in person (appointment required, and this cannot be done by an authorised representative on someone else's behalf) or completes a video call arranged through Netherlands Worldwide, the Ministry of Foreign Affairs' service for Dutch citizens abroad. During the video call or in-person appointment, a valid passport or national identity card is required for verification - a driving licence is explicitly not accepted as valid ID for this purpose.

If the collection code expires before it's used, the only option is to restart the application from the beginning to receive a new one. Separately, a DigiD that goes unused for three years or more automatically expires, at which point a fresh application at a service desk (not simply reactivation) is required, regardless of whether the person still remembers their original username and password.
