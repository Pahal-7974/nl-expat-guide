TITLE: Switching Dutch Health Insurers and the Healthcare Allowance (Zorgtoeslag)
SOURCE_URL: https://www.expatfocus.com/netherlands/guide/netherlands-health-insurance
SOURCE_URL_2: https://feather-insurance.com/en-nl/blog/health-insurance-netherlands-guide

Dutch health insurance policies run on an annual cycle: insurers publish their premiums and terms for the coming year each November, opening a switching window that runs through the end of December. A person can compare and move to a different insurer during this window, but they must cancel their existing policy before 31 December to make the switch effective for the new year - missing that cancellation deadline locks them into their current insurer for another full year, even though the formal switching deadline is sometimes described as extending into January.

For lower-income residents, the government offers a monthly subsidy called the zorgtoeslag (healthcare allowance) to offset part of the insurance premium. Eligibility and the amount depend on income and assets, and a person must already have valid Dutch health insurance before they can apply for it - there are no exceptions to this ordering. The allowance can meaningfully reduce the effective monthly cost of insurance for people with modest income, though it needs to be reapplied for or adjusted if income changes significantly during the year, since overpayments generally need to be paid back.

A separate point worth knowing: the Dutch basic insurance package only covers urgent medical care received abroad, and reimbursement for that care is capped at what the equivalent treatment would cost in the Netherlands - if treatment abroad costs more than the Dutch price, the difference is the patient's responsibility. Anyone who travels frequently or expects extended time outside the Netherlands should consider supplementary travel or international coverage on top of their Dutch basic policy, since the basic policy alone is not designed to fully cover care outside the country.
