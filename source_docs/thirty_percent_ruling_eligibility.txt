TITLE: 30% Ruling (Expat Tax Scheme) - Who Qualifies
SOURCE_URL: https://www.iamsterdam.com/en/live-work-study/living/official-procedures/30-tax-ruling
SOURCE_URL_2: https://business.gov.nl/staff/employing-staff/the-expat-scheme-30-percent-ruling-in-the-netherlands/

The 30% ruling, officially called the expat scheme, is a Dutch tax facility that lets an employer pay a portion of an eligible employee's gross salary as a tax-free allowance, meant to offset the extra costs of relocating for work (housing, travel, and similar expenses). As of 2026, the maximum tax-free share is still 30% of salary, but from 1 January 2027 this is scheduled to drop to 27% for the remaining duration of any active ruling.

To qualify, an employee generally needs to meet several conditions together: be recruited from abroad or transferred within a multinational company, have lived more than 150 kilometres (straight-line distance) from the Dutch border for at least 16 of the 24 months before their first Dutch working day, work under an employment contract with a Dutch payroll-registered employer, and meet a minimum taxable salary threshold. For 2026, that threshold is €48,013 per year (after the 30% deduction is applied), or a reduced €36,497 for employees under 30 who hold a qualifying master's degree. Scientific researchers at designated institutions and doctors training as specialists are exempt from the salary threshold entirely.

The 150km rule is enforced strictly and is one of the most common reasons applications get rejected - it effectively excludes people who were already living close to the Dutch border, including many residents of Belgium and parts of Germany, in the recent past.

Self-employed people and freelancers cannot use the 30% ruling directly, since it requires an employer to apply on the employee's behalf - a freelancer would need to set up a company and become that company's employee to be eligible.

The ruling can last up to five years from the first working day, and the eligible salary itself is capped: as of 1 January 2026, only salary up to €262,000 per year counts toward the tax-free calculation, in line with the Dutch Standard for Remuneration Act.
