TITLE: Highly Skilled Migrant Residence Permit - Overview
SOURCE_URL: https://ind.nl/en/residence-permits/work/highly-skilled-migrant
SOURCE_URL_2: https://ind.nl/en/about-us/background-articles/national-highly-skilled-migrant-scheme

To work in the Netherlands as a highly skilled migrant (kennismigrant), a foreign national needs a residence permit. Unlike most other work permits, this application cannot be submitted by the individual themselves - only an employer that the IND has officially recognised as a sponsor is allowed to file it on the employee's behalf.

The national highly skilled migrant scheme is the most commonly used route for employers bringing in international talent, aimed at supporting the Dutch knowledge economy. Recognised sponsors go through an IND vetting process that checks the company's reliability, continuity, and financial health before they're added to a public register (which currently lists roughly 10,000 companies). Being a recognised sponsor gives the employer access to a faster application track, but also comes with ongoing obligations: keeping records, notifying the IND of relevant changes, and a general duty of care toward the employee.

Alongside the national scheme, other routes exist for similar profiles - the European Blue Card, arrangements for scientific researchers, the Intra-Corporate Transfer (ICT) directive for staff moving within a multinational, and schemes for self-employed entrepreneurs.

If the employment relationship ends or changes such that the person no longer meets the conditions of the permit, the employer is required to inform the IND. A new employer taking on a highly skilled migrant already living in the Netherlands must also notify the IND within a set period so eligibility can be reassessed.
