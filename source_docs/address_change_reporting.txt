TITLE: Reporting a Change of Address After Moving Within the Netherlands
SOURCE_URL: https://www.government.nl/faq/how-do-i-inform-the-municipality-of-a-change-of-address
SOURCE_URL_2: https://www.iamexpat.nl/housing/dutch-housing-market/change-of-address-netherlands

Anyone already registered in the Netherlands who moves to a new address - whether within the same municipality or to a different one - must report the change to their new municipality. This is a separate, simpler process than a first-time registration from abroad: since the person already has a BSN, most municipalities let this be done entirely online via DigiD, rather than requiring an in-person appointment. There's no need to deregister from the old municipality first; the new municipality handles notifying the old one automatically as part of processing the update.

The reporting window is generous but strict at the edges: a move can be reported starting four weeks before the move date, and must be reported no later than five days after moving. If someone reports later than five days after the actual move, the date they submit the report becomes the official registered move-in date rather than the true move date, which can create a mismatch with other paperwork (like a rental contract's start date). Municipalities can fine up to €325 for failing to report a change of address within this window, and being incorrectly registered can also cause downstream problems with benefits, insurance, and tax records, since several other agencies pull address data from the same central registry.

Once the municipality processes the change, it automatically forwards the new address to several other organisations - typically including the Belastingdienst (tax authority), pension and social insurance bodies, and vehicle registration if applicable - so re-notifying each of them individually usually isn't necessary. However, this automatic notification does not extend to everything: banks, employers, health insurers, landlords/utility companies, and any organisation not connected to the national personal records database generally still need to be told separately. Processing time for the address update itself varies by municipality, commonly landing somewhere between about ten working days and three weeks.
