TITLE: What DigiD Is and How to Apply
SOURCE_URL: https://www.digid.nl/en/apply-and-activate/apply-digid
SOURCE_URL_2: https://www.iamexpat.nl/expat-info/official-issues/digid-netherlands

DigiD (Digital Identification) is the Netherlands' system for proving your identity online when dealing with government and government-linked organisations - effectively a digital replacement for showing a passport at a counter. It's used for filing taxes, applying for benefits and allowances, checking pension information, and interacting with hundreds of organisations including the IND and the Belastingdienst. A DigiD account is personal and tied to your BSN; it consists of a username, password, and (recommended, and often required by individual services) two-factor verification via SMS or the DigiD app.

To apply, someone needs to already have a BSN and be registered at a Dutch address in the BRP (the personal records database), plus a mobile phone that can receive SMS and an email address. The application itself is done online at digid.nl: fill in personal details (BSN, date of birth, postal code), choose security settings, and create a username and password. After submitting the online application, an activation letter containing a code is posted to the applicant's registered BRP address - this typically takes a few working days, and the specific window (often cited as 3 to 5 working days) depends on regional mail delivery. The activation code generally needs to be used within a limited window after being issued, so it shouldn't be left unused for long once it arrives.

People who are not yet living in the Netherlands, or who live abroad while still needing a DigiD (for example while their move is being processed), follow a different route: they apply online but then collect their activation code either at a physical DigiD service desk in the Netherlands or via a video call arranged through Netherlands Worldwide, rather than by post.

DigiD credentials are strictly personal and should never be shared - not with employers, not with accountants helping with a tax return. Where someone else needs to act on a person's behalf (for instance, an accountant filing taxes), the correct approach is a formal DigiD authorisation ("DigiD Machtigen"), not sharing login details directly.
